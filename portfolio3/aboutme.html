i’ve been chasing the best version of myself —
and for a while, i thought i saw it.
i started skating, fell more than i rolled,
but i loved the sound of the board hitting the ground.
i learned the drums, the electric guitar —
borrowed, not mine,
but the noise felt like progress.
maybe i was growing. maybe i was just pretending.

i kept saying i was fine,
and most people believed it.
only two saw through the cracks.
they made me wonder —
can i really change?
can i rebuild what’s broken inside me?
or am i just repainting the same old walls?

i talk more now,
smile more too,
but sometimes it feels rehearsed.
my confidence went up,
but my heart stayed quiet.
i tell myself i don’t care how i look —
but then again,
who even cares if i change?
who cares if i fail?

i wanted to feel alive,
but somewhere in trying to heal,
i forgot how to *feel* at all.
i wanted to grow —
but maybe growth isn’t always healing.
maybe it’s just the pain evolving with me.
